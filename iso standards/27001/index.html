    ISO 27001: Information Security Management System

    Welcome to the ISO 27001 documentation page. This page outlines the key principles, requirements, and processes for maintaining a compliant Information Security Management System (ISMS).

    1. Introduction

    ISO 27001 is an international standard for managing information security. It provides a framework for organizations to protect sensitive information, manage risks, and ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data.

    2. Scope

    ISO 27001 applies to any organization that handles information requiring protection. Key areas include:

    • Risk management and assessment
    • Security policies and procedures
    • Access control and data protection
    • Compliance with legal and regulatory requirements

    3. Key Principles

    ISO 27001 focuses on several core principles:

    1. Confidentiality Ensuring information is accessible only to those authorized.
    2. Integrity Safeguarding the accuracy and completeness of information.
    3. Availability Ensuring authorized users have access to information when needed.
    4. Risk management Identifying, assessing, and mitigating security risks.
    5. Continuous improvement Regularly updating the ISMS to address new threats.

    4. Opportunities & Risks

    Organizations must identify potential opportunities to strengthen information security and risks that could compromise it. Examples include:

    • Opportunities:
    • Implementing multi-factor authentication
    • Conducting regular security awareness training
    • Risks:
    • Cyberattacks or data breaches
    • Unauthorized access to confidential data

    5. Documentation & Records

    ISO 27001 requires maintaining documented information to support the ISMS:

    • Information security policies
    • Procedures for incident management and business continuity
    • Records of risk assessments, audits, and corrective actions

    6. Continual Improvement

    A robust ISMS is never static. Organizations are expected to:

    • Monitor security controls and processes
    • Conduct internal audits
    • Review incidents and lessons learned
    • Implement corrective and preventive actions
