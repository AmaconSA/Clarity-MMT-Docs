    ISO 45001: Occupational Health and Safety Management System

    Welcome to the ISO 45001 documentation page. This page outlines the key principles, requirements, and processes for maintaining a compliant Occupational Health and Safety Management System (OHSMS).

    1. Introduction

    ISO 45001 is an international standard for occupational health and safety. It provides a framework for organizations to prevent work-related injuries, improve employee safety, and ensure a safe working environment.

    2. Scope

    ISO 45001 applies to organizations of all sizes and industries. Key areas include:

    • Risk assessment and hazard identification
    • Safety policies and procedures
    • Employee training and awareness
    • Compliance with legal and regulatory requirements

    3. Key Principles

    ISO 45001 emphasizes several core principles:

    1. Leadership and commitment – Management must actively lead safety initiatives.
    2. Worker participation – Employees should be involved in health and safety decisions.
    3. Hazard identification and risk control – Proactively prevent incidents.
    4. Continual improvement – Regularly monitor, review, and improve the OHSMS.
    5. Compliance with legal requirements – Ensure all applicable laws and regulations are met.

    4. Opportunities & Risks

    Organizations should identify opportunities to improve safety and risks that could lead to accidents. Examples include:

    • Opportunities:
    • Implementing ergonomic workplace solutions
    • Conducting regular safety drills
    • Risks:
    • Workplace accidents or near-misses
    • Non-compliance with safety regulations

    5. Documentation & Records

    ISO 45001 requires maintaining documented information to support the OHSMS:

    • Health and safety policies
    • Risk assessments and hazard logs
    • Records of training, audits, and corrective actions
    • Incident reports and investigations

    6. Continual Improvement

    A strong OHSMS requires ongoing effort. Organizations are expected to:

    • Monitor safety performance indicators
    • Conduct internal safety audits
    • Review incidents and feedback
    • Implement corrective and preventive actions
